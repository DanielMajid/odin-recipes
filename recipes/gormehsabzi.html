Gormeh Sabzi

This dish is the national dish of Iran.

Served with Tahdig and rice it is perhaps, the national dish of all Persian peoples throughout the diaspora. Unlike some other dishes that stir controversy, Gormeh Sabzi is universally beloved for its complex flavors, and its hearty belly-filling nature. For non persians it might be an acquired taste, but give it time. You will see the light. And like any good stew, it only gets more delicious as the days pass since its preparation. Gormeh Sabzi on day three is goated.

Ingredients

serves 6
For the stew
  • 2 lbs. cubed boneless lamb or beef (can be omitted to make vegetarian)
  • 4 bunches italian parsley (cleaned and chopped)
  • 2 bunches cilantro (cleaned and chopped)
  • 2 leeks (cleaned and chopped)
  • 1 medium yellow onion diced
  • 2 teaspoons ground tumeric
  • 3 dried persian limes
  • 1/2 cup plus 2 tbls olive oil
  • 3/4 cup dried herb melange
  • 2 cups water
  • salt to taste
  • black pepper to taste
  • juice of 1/2 a lemon

Dried Spice Melange

They who control the spice, controls the universe!

This melange is something that can bend and fold and change to your taste and preferences. Keep in mind that herbs make up 90% of this dish. Try this blend and see where your heart leads you.

  • 1/2 cup dried gormeh sabzi herb mix
  • 1/4 cup dried fenugreek
  • 1/8 cup dried crushed mint
  • 1/8 cup dried dill

Preparation

  1. Heat a wide skillet on medium heat and add the chopped herbs. Cook these to release the moisture. Stir constantly once the herbs have given up most of the moisture. Continue to mix until the pan begins to look dry and then in batches add a little olive oil at a time while stirring until 1/2 cup has been added and is well incorporated. Remove from heat and set aside
  2. Add one tbls olive oil to another wide lidded skillet or dutch oven and saute onions with a pinch of salt until translucent. Add tumeric and cook for one minute while stirring. Remove from the pan.
